# Finish Plan — "Toxic Green" 944 EV

Make the car a vivid, luminous lime-green inspired by the **Moog Little Phatty "Toxic"** synth
(only 30 made). Twin preview color: **`#62E32B`** (tunable in the CAR tab's Paint picker to match a
real sprayout/sample). Decision: ADR-0017.

## The color
- **Toxic Green** — bright acid/highlighter lime, gloss. **Not a factory Porsche color**, so it's a
  **custom mix or wrap**. Real-world matches to chase a sprayout against: House of Kolor *Lime Time*,
  PPG *Hot Lime / Vibrance*, Axalta *Solar Bright*, or a wrap like Avery *Grass Green* / 3M *Light Green*.
- High-vis greens are **low-hide pigments** — they need a **white/ground coat** for even, glowing
  coverage (paint or wrap). Budget for that step; it's why neon colors cost more.

## Route A — Vinyl wrap (recommended first)
- **Cost:** ~$3–4k pro · ~$600–1,200 DIY (materials) for a 944.
- **Pros:** reversible (great on a project car), protects the underlying paint, easy exact-color match,
  gloss/satin/matte options, faster, no booth. Good way to **live with the color** before committing.
- **Cons:** ~5–7 yr life, edges/door-jambs need care, not as deep as paint up close.

## Route B — Respray (paint)
- **Cost:** ~$4–9k+ for a quality 2-stage custom mix (more for show-level).
- **Pros:** permanent, deepest look, best durability, paints jambs/edges properly.
- **Cons:** full prep + booth + downtime; custom-mix neon is pricey; harder to undo.

## Prep (either route — a 39-yr-old shell)
1. **Bodywork first:** address rust (944 common spots: sunroof, battery tray, hatch, sills), dents,
   stone chips. Paint/wrap only hides so much.
2. Remove/again-mask trim, lights, badges, bumpers; wrap needs clean edges, paint needs jambs done.
3. Sand → prime → **white ground coat** → color → clear (paint) ‖ or wrap over a flawless surface.
4. Reinstall trim; detail.

## Where it fits in the build (don't let it block "it drives")
Cosmetics are a **post-MVP** milestone — get it **driving first** (in primer/black), then finish.
- **Sequence:** mechanical MVP (drives, brakes, charges) → bodywork → Toxic Green wrap/paint → trim.
- Doing it after the drivetrain is in avoids re-masking around HV work and lets you fix any panel
  damage from the conversion before final color.

## Accents that work with Toxic Green
- Keep the **black wheels** (high contrast, looks aggressive) — already the plan.
- **Black or carbon** mirrors/trim/spoiler; a thin **black or dark-grey stripe** optional.
- The app's **cyan pop-up glow** + toxic-green body is a great synthwave combo for the logo/splash.

## Realistic cost/time add to the project
- **Wrap:** ~$600–4k, ~1 week (DIY longer); **Paint:** ~$4–9k, 2–4 weeks of body+booth time.
- Tracked as a cosmetic task on the PLAN board (parallel/after DRIVE).
