# ADR-0011: Strip to reinvest — saved money & weight feed the drivetrain

**Status:** Accepted
**Date:** 2026-06-27
**Deciders:** Travis

## Context
Every part removed (ICE ~560 lb, comfort ~200 lb) frees **both weight and money** (parts sold +
parts not bought). The goal, stated plainly: **that savings goes into the drivetrain — more
battery (range) or a more powerful motor.** The motor (front) and the kept transaxle (rear)
anchor the ~50/50 balance; the battery is placed to hold it. This is *the* objective; everything
else (comfort, trim) is fat to be cut and converted into go.

## Decision
**Strip the fat, feed the muscle.** Reinvest all saved money and weight into the powertrain —
maximize battery (and/or motor) **up to ≈ stock weight at true 50/50.** Stripping ~760 lb *buys*
the weight budget to carry **more** battery than a non-stripped conversion would at the same
final weight, and the **sold gas parts fund the cells.**

## Alternatives considered
- **Minimize everything, incl. the pack (pure featherweight)** — rejected: that *banks* the
  freed weight/money instead of spending it on range/power. The savings are meant to be reinvested.
- **Keep the comfort gear and add a big pack anyway** — rejected: blows past stock weight, hurts
  balance and handling, and costs more.

## Consequences
- **Positive:** stripping **buys range** (~3 extra Tesla modules at the same weight as a
  non-stripped build); self-funding (sold parts pay for cells); stays ≈ stock weight at true
  50/50; a sharper car.
- **Negative / accepted cost:** spartan (no comfort); and the **kept transaxle caps usable motor
  power** (ADR-0004) — past the EM57's output, extra dollars buy more **battery (range)** with a
  better return than a bigger motor. A genuinely *more powerful* motor would eventually argue for
  the Tier-3 / direct-drive path (a much bigger change).
- **Follow-ups:** weight-budget worksheet (`battery-pack-and-balance.md`); front-bias the module
  split; corner-balance at commissioning.
