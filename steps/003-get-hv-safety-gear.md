# Step 003 — Get HV safety gear (PF4)

**Phase:** NOW · **Task:** #4 · **Gate:** PF4 · **Cost:** ~$300–600 (one-time, reusable)
**Blocked by:** nothing
**Blocks:** 012 (any live HV work)

## Do
- [ ] Acquire: **Class-0 (1000 V) gloves** + leather overgloves, **CAT III DC meter** (≥600 V),
      **insulation tester (megger)**, **DC-rated fire extinguisher**, insulated tools.
- [ ] Read `../SECURITY.md` end to end.
- [ ] **Rehearse the de-energize + live-dead-live procedure** (practice on the donor when you have it).

## Done when
Gear is in hand and you can perform the de-energize procedure from memory.

## Refs
**`../SECURITY.md`** · `../docs/hv-bom.md` (§ PPE)

## Notes
- The **one place never to cheap out** below the rating. This is your life, not a line item.
- Budget brands are fine *if they meet CAT III / Class 0*.

## Tools
- Class-0 (1000 V) insulated gloves + leather over-gloves
- Insulated 1000 V tools
- CAT III/IV multimeter with good leads
- Face shield + a CO₂/Class-C fire extinguisher
- Insulated rescue hook; a non-conductive mat

## Time & difficulty
1–2 days (shipping) · easy

## ⚠ Safety
- This gear is what stands between you and a 300+ V DC arc. Don't cheap out.

## Tips & gotchas
- Inspect/air-test the gloves before **every** use; store them rolled, out of sunlight.
- Adopt the **one-hand rule** working near HV; keep the other in a pocket.
- Write your **verify-dead** procedure on a card and tape it to the wall.

## Avoid
- Generic 'electrician' gloves — they must be rated + in-date.
- Working HV alone or when tired.
